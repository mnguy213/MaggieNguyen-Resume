I am currently an undergraduate student at the University of California Riverside majoring in Biology. Prior to my education at UCR, I attended Bolsa Grande High School where I held various positions in esteemed school organizations.

Holding the title of Cabinet Commissioner for the Associative Student Body (ASB) and Public Relations Officer for the Protective Animal Welfare Society Club (PAWS) on campus helped me acquire many necessary skills for future endeavors. Being a part of ASB helped me learn the infrastructures of planning any event, such as a school dance or a fundraiser. Understanding how to budget and brainstorm decorations and create schedules and setups was something that was completely new to me at the time. However, it really made me aware of all of the behind the scenes work that goes into planning, not just these events, but through daily life in general. Working in the student store also accustomed me to practice my patience and develop my ability to work under pressure, as it does get hectic and demanding at times. In PAWS, I performed similar tasks in assisting the scheduling and organization of fundraisers and club events.

I have been a part of the Vietnamese Eucharistic Youth Ministry at my church for the last 14 years. I grew up in this organization and I always knew that I would become a youth leader myself. Taking inspiration from my own youth leaders that taught me, I constantly try to make my lessons as inclusive and interactive as possible. Creating these lessons can prove to be a challenge at time, as we are required to tie in modern teachings with biblical themes. However, this challenge propelled me to think outside of the box more and push the boundaries.
